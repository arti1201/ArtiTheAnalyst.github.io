Project Overview

This project focused on applying key business analysis tools and techniques to diagnose, map, and improve inefficient business processes. The goal was to simulate a real-world consulting experience — identifying process bottlenecks and delivering strategic solutions through root cause analysis and visual modeling.

What I Did

Root Cause Analysis

  • Applied the Five Whys technique to uncover the underlying causes of recurring process issues.
  • Investigated a customer onboarding process with delays and miscommunications across departments.
  • Identified critical inefficiencies in handoff procedures between sales and operations.

Process Modeling

  • Designed Lucidchart process maps to visualize existing workflows and proposed improvements.
  • Created both "As-Is" and "To-Be" models, helping stakeholders understand gaps and proposed changes.
  • Mapped decision points, data flow, and interdepartmental dependencies to clarify responsibility.

Challenges Faced

  • Unclear Ownership: The process involved multiple teams with vague handoffs, leading to finger- pointing and delays.
    • Solution: Clearly defined each team’s roles and responsibilities in the updated workflow model.
  • Hidden Inefficiencies:Stakeholders were unaware of the small delays stacking up to major inefficiencies.
    • Solution: Quantified the impact of each delay using simple Excel-based time tracking and demonstrated how streamlining would cut lead time by 30%.
  • Resistance to Change: Some team members were skeptical of the changes due to unfamiliarity with modeling tools.
    • Solution: Presented before-and-after process flows in Lucidchart during a simulated stakeholder meeting to demonstrate clarity and value.

Impact & Outcome

  • Produced a comprehensive business process improvement plan backed by visual evidence and root cause logic.
  • The visual models facilitated better cross-functional alignment and communication in simulated stakeholder reviews.
  • Gained hands-on experience in business process optimization, stakeholder analysis, and professional documentation.

Takeaways

  • Strengthened my understanding of business analysis frameworks such as SIPOC, Five Whys, and process mapping.
  • Learned how to convert stakeholder pain points into actionable solutions.
  • Improved my ability to visually communicate complex ideas using professional tools like Lucidchart.
